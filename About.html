About me

My name is Peder Ormar, and this website is about me! Here you can get to know me a bit better. I am a student who has a dream to become a soccer commentator, and make a living from watching sports.

I come from a little town in Norway called Sandefjord — born and raised in the “Whale City”. From age 6 up until I was 19 years old I played for one of the soccer teams in the city, Store Bergan IL.

I stopped playing for them when I was 19, because of military service. After one year at the Norway–Russia border, I moved back to Sandefjord and played soccer while working at an elementary school to make money.

After 6 months of working, I decided it was time to study. Since I always wanted to be a sports commentator, I figured a bachelor in journalism would be the right choice for me. And after one year in Norway I am now here in the US, making this website as an assignment. Weird how things work out, huh?

Fun Facts

I love sports

Especially soccer — both watching and playing. Following leagues and games is a big part of my life.

Military experience

I served one year on the Norway–Russia border, which taught me discipline and teamwork.

Future goals

My dream is to work as a professional sports commentator and travel the world through sports.

My proudest moment in journalism

One of the highlights of my early journalism journey was interviewing Petter Northug during the Ski World Championships in Trondheim. As someone who dreams of working in sports media and football commentary, this moment showed me that I’m on the right path.

Being on site, preparing questions and then standing in front of the camera with one of Norway’s biggest sports profiles was both nerve-racking and motivating. It gave me a taste of what I want to do more of in the future.
